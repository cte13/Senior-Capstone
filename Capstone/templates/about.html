About Us

This website has been created by three Informatics majors studying at the University of Washington. We are Jeff Cairns, Misha Tsao and Cory Eurom.

A "Capstone Project" is a six-month project which requires groups of 3-5 students to solve an information problem. This information problem is all about gathering, organizing and analyzing data.

In the wake of events such as Lehman Brothers declaring bankruptcy and Bernie Madoff having the perfect name, the federal government has mandated increased transparency throughout the financial sector. Legislation like the Dodd-Frank Act require companies to submit nearly every piece of financial information. This makes investing safe again. That is, if you take advantage of the data.

XBRL is a machine-readable language. Imagine you are the Securities and Exchange Commissions and you legislate every company in the US to submit data to you. There are no technical specifications to the format of the data therefore anything is accepted. Data flows in like a tornado from Kansas: scanned accounting papers, JPEGs, PDFs, XLSX, TXT, anything! Introducing XBRL: The future of financial reporting. The eXtensible Business Reporting Language is an extension of XML and it follows a very similar format. Using open-source software, this website collects data from XBRL-tagged documents then allows users to filter and download the data. Our goal for this website is to put BlackRock at a competitive advantage over the market.

Here is the order of operations for this website:

  1. Companies submit financial reports to the SEC
  2. The reports are made available to the public
  3. We collect Form Ds and Form 497s (Form 485BPOS in some cases) for analysis
  4. We parse the forms and extract the pertinent data
  5. The website reads the database then users can filter, sort and analyze

We would like to thank Marisa Quinn, Eric Larsen and Doug Cairns of BlackRock Inc. for providing us this opportunity.
